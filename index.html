"Your Wallet Is Not a Charity"

Ever been guilt-tripped into tipping? Pressured to give money to someone who didn't earn it? That ends now. Learn how to stop being a financial doormat and start keeping what's yours.

Life's Not a Tip Jar

Here's a fun fact: Life doesn't give out tips for doing the bare minimum. I know, I know—it's shocking. But if you're standing there, staring at your screen while someone asks you for a tip for "helping" them with something they were already paid to do, don't feel guilty for saying no.

They're not entitled to your cash because they pressed a button or handed you your coffee with a fake smile.

If your job is to serve me, guess what? Your paycheck is for doing your job. The tip? That's for going above and beyond. If you think you're going to get a bonus for doing the minimum, then congratulations—you've misunderstood the basic principle of capitalism. You're not entitled to a damn thing.

Hard Earners: The People Who Actually Do Things

Here's a little secret about us hard earners: We don't ask for handouts. We don't beg for tips. We don't sit around waiting for someone to "give us a break" or bail us out of our mess. We get up, do the work, and make something of ourselves.

So when the entitled people start looking at us like we owe them, we just smile. Because we know they're the ones with the problem, not us.

And if you're one of the people who thinks they deserve money for doing absolutely nothing, I've got a question for you: Why do you think anyone would reward your laziness?
